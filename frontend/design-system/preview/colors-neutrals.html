Why neutrals stay put. The scale ramps from #FFFFFF through warm-cool grays to near-black #111827 — the hue cast across all eight steps is intentionally neutral (a hint of cool blue at #F8F8FB, but barely perceivable). Because neutrals carry almost no chroma, they sit cleanly under any accent: the violet system used them, the blue system uses them, and a future swap wouldn't need them re-tuned. Re-tinting toward the accent (e.g. blue-leaning grays) would actually weaken the accent — the contrast between --c-accent and the surface comes from chroma difference, not hue match.
